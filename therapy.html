What is TCM? The Basics

Think of TCM as "preventive maintenance" for your body, similar to how you maintain a car or computer system.

⚡

Qi (Energy)

Western equivalent: Bioelectricity + Metabolism

Just as electricity flows through circuits in your home, Qi flows through pathways (meridians) in your body. When the flow is blocked, systems malfunction.

☯

Yin & Yang

Western equivalent: Homeostasis + Balance

Like your body maintaining 98.6°F temperature or balancing pH levels. TCM tracks broader balances: activity vs rest, heat vs cold, stimulation vs calm.

🌿

Five Elements

Western equivalent: Organ Systems Interactions

Similar to how your endocrine, nervous, and immune systems interact. TCM maps these relationships through five categories: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water.

Treatment Methods

Evidence-based approaches with modern scientific backing

📍 Acupuncture Points

Acupuncture

Precision nerve stimulation therapy

🔬 The Science

Needles stimulate specific nerve bundles and fascia (connective tissue), triggering:

  • Release of endorphins (natural painkillers)
  • Activation of the parasympathetic nervous system (rest & digest mode)
  • Improved blood flow and tissue oxygenation
  • Regulation of inflammatory cytokines

✓ Evidence-Based Applications

Chronic Pain Migraines Stress/Anxiety Insomnia Nausea (incl. chemotherapy) Arthritis

🎯 What to Expect

Ultra-fine needles (0.12-0.35mm) inserted at specific points. Most patients feel minimal sensation—sometimes a dull ache or tingling. Sessions last 20-40 minutes. Many feel immediate relaxation; pain relief typically builds over 3-6 sessions.

🌿 Herbal Formulas

Herbal Medicine

Phytochemical precision formulas

🔬 The Science

TCM herbs contain active compounds that function similarly to pharmaceutical drugs, but in complex synergistic combinations:

  • Ginseng: Adaptogenic compounds (ginsenosides) regulate cortisol and energy metabolism
  • Astragalus: Polysaccharides enhance immune cell activity
  • Ginger: Gingerols reduce inflammation and modulate serotonin (anti-nausea)
  • Licorice Root: Glycyrrhizin has cortisol-like anti-inflammatory effects

✓ Evidence-Based Applications

Immune Support Digestive Issues Hormonal Balance Chronic Fatigue Skin Conditions Respiratory Health

⚠️ Safety & Quality

All herbs are pharmaceutical-grade, tested for heavy metals and contaminants. Formulas are customized based on your specific pattern diagnosis—unlike one-size-fits-all supplements.

🔴 Cupping Therapy

Cupping Therapy

Negative pressure tissue therapy

🔬 The Science

Suction cups create negative pressure on skin and underlying tissues:

  • Increases local blood circulation (hyperemia)
  • Promotes lymphatic drainage
  • Releases fascial adhesions (similar to deep tissue massage)
  • Triggers mechanotransduction (cellular response to mechanical pressure)

✓ Evidence-Based Applications

Muscle Pain Back Tension Sports Recovery Respiratory Congestion

🎯 What to Expect

Cups are placed on skin for 5-15 minutes, creating circular marks (not bruises—these are petechiae from surface capillaries). Marks fade in 3-7 days. Sensation is like a deep tissue massage.

🔥 Moxibustion

Moxibustion

Thermal infrared therapy

🔬 The Science

Burning mugwort (Artemisia vulgaris) near acupuncture points produces:

  • Infrared radiation (penetrates 8-10mm into tissue)
  • Heat shock protein activation (cellular repair)
  • Increased local blood flow
  • Volatile oil compounds with anti-inflammatory properties

✓ Evidence-Based Applications

Chronic Cold Conditions Digestive Issues Joint Stiffness Breech Baby (research-supported)

The Diagnostic Process

How TCM practitioners assess your condition

1

Comprehensive Interview

Detailed health history covering sleep, digestion, stress, pain patterns, and lifestyle—similar to a functional medicine intake.

2

Tongue Analysis

The tongue reflects internal organ health and circulation. Changes in coating, color, and shape provide diagnostic clues (similar to how doctors examine the throat or eyes).

3

Pulse Diagnosis

Three finger positions on each wrist assess 12 organ systems. Practitioners detect qualities like "wiry" (tension), "slippery" (inflammation), or "weak" (deficiency)—comparable to checking heart rhythm variations.

4

Pattern Differentiation

Unlike Western medicine's "one disease, one treatment" approach, TCM identifies your unique pattern. Two people with migraines might receive completely different treatments based on their individual patterns.

Common Questions

Is TCM safe?

When practiced by licensed professionals, TCM has an excellent safety record. We use single-use sterile needles, pharmaceutical-grade herbs, and follow strict protocols. Always inform your practitioner of medications and conditions.

How many sessions will I need?

Acute conditions (recent injuries) may resolve in 1-3 sessions. Chronic conditions typically require 6-12 sessions for lasting change. We assess progress every 4 sessions and adjust accordingly.

Can TCM work with my medications?

Yes. TCM is complementary medicine—we work alongside your conventional treatments. Some herbs may interact with medications, which is why we take a complete health history and coordinate with your physician when needed.

Is it painful?

Acupuncture needles are hair-thin (0.12-0.35mm)—about 1/40th the size of a hypodermic needle. Most patients feel nothing or a brief dull sensation. Many find sessions deeply relaxing and fall asleep.
