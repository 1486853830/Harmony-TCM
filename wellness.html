Core Principles

The foundation of TCM wellness philosophy

☯

Balance is Everything

Health is the harmonious balance of yin and yang energies. When one becomes dominant, illness follows. TCM works to restore equilibrium.

Example: Too much stress (yang) → need rest and meditation (yin)
⚡

Qi Flow

Vital energy (Qi) must flow freely through meridians. Blockages cause pain and disease. Treatments remove obstacles and restore flow.

Example: Shoulder tension → acupuncture releases blocked Qi
🌿

Five Elements

Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water represent different organs, emotions, and seasons. Understanding these connections guides personalized treatment.

Example: Spring (Wood) → focus on liver health and anger management
🔄

Prevention First

TCM emphasizes preventing illness before it manifests. Small imbalances are addressed before they become serious conditions.

Example: Seasonal adjustments → prevent colds and flu

Understanding Your Body Type

TCM identifies different constitutional types. Knowing yours helps personalize your wellness approach.

🔥

Hot Type

Characteristics:

  • Feel warm easily
  • Red face, prone to acne
  • Thirsty, prefer cold drinks
  • Constipation, dark urine
  • Restless sleep

Wellness Tips:

  • Eat cooling foods (cucumber, watermelon)
  • Avoid spicy and fried foods
  • Drink green tea and chrysanthemum tea
  • Practice calming meditation
  • Get adequate sleep
❄️

Cold Type

Characteristics:

  • Feel cold easily
  • Pale complexion
  • Prefers warm drinks
  • Loose stools, frequent urination
  • Lethargic, low energy

Wellness Tips:

  • Eat warming foods (ginger, cinnamon)
  • Include soups and stews
  • Drink ginger tea and ginseng tea
  • Exercise regularly to boost circulation
  • Keep warm, especially feet and abdomen
💧

Damp Type

Characteristics:

  • Heaviness in body
  • Swelling, especially in limbs
  • Sticky mouth, poor appetite
  • Loose stools, bloating
  • Weight gain easily

Wellness Tips:

  • Eat drying foods (mung beans, job's tears)
  • Avoid dairy, sweets, and greasy foods
  • Drink barley tea and lotus leaf tea
  • Exercise to promote sweating
  • Use saunas or hot baths
🍂

Dry Type

Characteristics:

  • Dry skin, hair, and nails
  • Dry cough, thirsty
  • Constipation
  • Thin body type
  • Insomnia, anxiety

Wellness Tips:

  • Eat moisturizing foods (pear, honey, sesame)
  • Include soups and broths
  • Drink goji berry and longan tea
  • Use humidifier in dry weather
  • Practice gentle, restorative yoga
😴

Qi Deficiency

Characteristics:

  • Easily fatigued
  • Shortness of breath
  • Weak voice
  • Poor digestion
  • Frequent illness

Wellness Tips:

  • Eat qi-tonifying foods (ginseng, astragalus)
  • Include warm, cooked meals
  • Drink ginseng and jujube tea
  • Get adequate rest and sleep
  • Avoid overexertion
🩸

Blood Deficiency

Characteristics:

  • Pale complexion, lips
  • Dizziness, blurred vision
  • Pale nails
  • Palpitations
  • Insomnia, poor memory

Wellness Tips:

  • Eat blood-nourishing foods (red dates, black sesame)
  • Include iron-rich foods
  • Drink goji berry and red date tea
  • Practice gentle exercise
  • Get regular, adequate sleep

Daily Wellness Routine

A simple TCM-inspired daily routine for optimal health

6:00 AM

Morning Awakening

Wake up naturally or gently. Drink warm water with lemon to stimulate digestion and hydrate your body.

🌅 Stretch gently 💧 Hydrate 🧘 Light meditation
7:00 AM

Nourishing Breakfast

Warm, cooked breakfast is best. Congee with herbs or warm oatmeal with goji berries provides sustained energy.

🍲 Warm porridge 🫖 Herbal tea 🥣 Nutritious
12:00 PM

Midday Nourishment

Eat your largest meal when digestion is strongest. Include a variety of vegetables, proteins, and grains.

🥗 Balanced meal 🍵 Green tea 😌 Rest briefly
3:00 PM

Afternoon Revitalization

Herbal tea break. Choose based on your needs: ginseng for energy, chrysanthemum for cooling, or goji for nourishment.

🍵 Herbal tea 🚶 Short walk 💆 Stretch
6:00 PM

Evening Nourishment

Lighter dinner, eaten at least 3 hours before bed. Soups and lightly cooked vegetables are ideal.

🍲 Light soup 🥬 Vegetables ⏰ Early dinner
9:00 PM

Wind Down

Begin preparing for sleep. Turn off screens, dim lights, and engage in calming activities like reading or gentle stretching.

📖 Read 🧘 Meditate 🌙 No screens
10:00 PM

Restful Sleep

Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep. This is when your body repairs and restores. Keep your bedroom cool and dark.

😴 Quality sleep ❄️ Cool room 🌙 Dark environment

Seasonal Wellness

Align your wellness routine with nature's rhythms

🌸

Spring

Wood Element • Liver & Gallbladder

Focus:

Cleanse and renew. Spring is the time for detoxification and supporting liver health.

Recommended Foods:

  • Leafy greens
  • Sprouts
  • Sour flavors
  • Green tea

Wellness Tips:

  • Get outdoors and exercise
  • Manage anger and frustration
  • Practice gentle stretching
☀️

Summer

Fire Element • Heart & Small Intestine

Focus:

Expand and energize. Summer is about joy, activity, and supporting heart health.

Recommended Foods:

  • Cooling fruits
  • Leafy greens
  • Bitter flavors
  • Mint tea

Wellness Tips:

  • Stay hydrated
  • Practice joyful activities
  • Get moderate exercise
🍂

Autumn

Metal Element • Lungs & Large Intestine

Focus:

Harvest and let go. Autumn is about releasing what no longer serves you and supporting lung health.

Recommended Foods:

  • Pears and apples
  • Root vegetables
  • Pungent flavors
  • Chrysanthemum tea

Wellness Tips:

  • Practice breathing exercises
  • Let go of emotional baggage
  • Protect against cold
❄️

Winter

Water Element • Kidneys & Bladder

Focus:

Rest and restore. Winter is for conserving energy, building reserves, and supporting kidney health.

Recommended Foods:

  • Warming soups
  • Black beans
  • Salty flavors
  • Goji berry tea

Wellness Tips:

  • Get adequate rest
  • Practice meditation
  • Keep warm

Quick Wellness Tips

Simple habits for everyday health

01

Drink Warm Water

Start your day with warm water. It aids digestion, improves circulation, and helps your body eliminate toxins.

02

Eat Seasonally

Choose foods that are in season. They're more nutritious and aligned with your body's needs.

03

Practice Mindful Eating

Eat slowly, chew thoroughly, and focus on your food. This improves digestion and nutrient absorption.

04

Move Your Body

Regular exercise promotes Qi flow. Choose activities you enjoy and can maintain consistently.

05

Manage Stress

Chronic stress disrupts Qi flow. Practice meditation, deep breathing, or other stress-reduction techniques.

06

Get Quality Sleep

Sleep is when your body repairs. Aim for 7-8 hours and maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
